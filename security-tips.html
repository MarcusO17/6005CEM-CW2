Security Tips

Your security is important to us! Follow these guidelines to help protect your information and ensure a safe experience while using our appointment booking system.

The guide was last updated on 10 November 2024.
  • Use a password with at least 8 characters, including uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters (e.g, !, @, #).
  • Avoid common words, easily guessed information (like your name or birth date), or repeating your password across different sites.
  • Consider updating your password regularly (e.g., every 6 months) for added security.
  • Phishing is a technique used by attackers to trick you into revealing personal information. Be cautious with emails or messages that seem suspicious.
  • Do not click on links or download attachments from emails claiming to be from our system if they seem unusual.
  • Always verify any unexpected communication by contacting us through our official website or support center.
  • Check that the web address begins with https:// whenever you log in to our system. The "https" indicates a secure, encrypted connection.
  • Avoid logging in from public or shared devices when possible. If you must, always remember to log out when youâ€™re finished.
  • We highly recommend enabling two-factor authentication (2FA). 2FA adds an extra layer of security by requiring a code sent to your phone or email in addition to your password.
  • This additional step can help prevent unauthorized access to your account even if your password is compromised.
  • Review and update your personal information as needed within our system, but do so carefully to avoid accidental exposure of sensitive data.
  • Report any incorrect or suspicious information in your account to our support team immediately.
  • Try to avoid accessing your account over public Wi-Fi networks, as these are more susceptible to attacks.
  • If you must use a public network, consider using a Virtual Private Network (VPN) to secure your internet connection.
  • Regularly check your account activity and look for any unusual behavior, such as unauthorized bookings or changes to your information.
  • If you notice anything suspicious, reset your password immediately and contact our support team.

If you suspect any security issues or encounter a suspicious message or website claiming to be associated with eDoc Hospital Appointment Management System, please report it to us at support@edoc.com.

For more information on keeping your online accounts secure, visit these resources:

  • Federal Trade Commission Online Security
  • StaySafeOnline by the National Cybersecurity Alliance

By following these security tips, you can help protect your information and keep your experience with eDoc Hospital Appointment Booking System safe and secure.
